Research Description

Numerical Analysis Topics

In scientific and engineering, simulations based on partial differential equations (PDE) are widely used among mechanics, physics and portfolio investment. PDEs with discontinuous coefficients on several sub-domains separated by curve ‘interface’ are getting attention in real world application, e.g. modeling displacement of an elastic plane with a circular inclusion made by dissimilar materials. To handle these so called ‘interface’ problems, Immersed Finite Element Method (Z.Li, 1998) was proposed.

As shown in the below figure, the whole domain is separated by red interface. Classical Finite Element method constructs ‘body-fitting’ mesh (left) to fit the interface. Under such treatment, every element lies in one of the subdomains. However, Immersed Finite Element method just lets the interface pass through elements and constructs basis functions directly on standard Cartesian mesh. This is so-called non-body-fitting mesh (right) which saves computational resource of classical method. To adapt to interface, basis functions on elements with interface are ‘immersed’.

During the past two years’ research, I focused on scenario that number of subdomains is larger than 2. In this case, all interfaces may gather at points named triple junction points. The existence of these points leads to lack of Degree-of-Freedom when generating local interpolation system. This is the main obstacle in developing efficient numerical methods for multi-domain interface problems. Both spirits of Immersed Finite Element method and Partial Penalized Immersed Finite Element Method (T.Lin et al, 2015) are refined to adapt this new scenario. The two diagrams below show numerical solution of specific 2D elliptic interface problems under condition of multi-domain & triple junction points and discontinuous jump condition respectively.

Currently, I’m working on Elastodynamics Interface problems, i.e. the time-dependent Elastic Interface problems. Elastodynamics has wide applications in earthquake wave modeling. In the project, we consider bi-marterial Elastodynamic problem with interface. We propose a full discrete Partially Penalized Immersed Finite Element scheme and conduct a-priori error estimate for this numerical method.

Environmental Science Topics

In Environmental Science area, applied mathematics tools and statistical techniques are also employed to conduct computation and analysis. To model trend of water flow and transportation of pollutants, simulation tools based on PDEs are commonly used. By utilizing Finite Element and Finite Volume method, predictions and early detections can also be made. The diagram below shows kinetic energy density distribution in Binhu Network, Lake Taihu, China under condition of a water transferring project from 2018.8.1 to 2018.8.31. Density values along the river are calculated using a Finite Volume Algorithm. Besides such simulation tool, we can also leverage information from experimental data using statistical methods. For example, Logarithmic Mean Divisia Index (LMDI) model is widely used to decompose the evolution of environmental indicators within a long period of time. The decomposition results enable us to identify key influence factors (both drivers and resistances), therefore helping with environmental policy-making process. In the past two years, I focused on these techniques to quantify relationship between environmental impacts and human behavior for integrated solutions of sustainability development.
