Research Description

Numerical Analysis Topics

In scientific research, simulations based on partial differential equations (PDE) are used widely among mechanics, physics and engineering application. PDEs with discontinuous coefficients on several sub-domains separated by curves are getting attention in real world application. Immersed Finite Element Methods (Z.Li, 1998) were proposed to handle these so called ‘interface problems’.

In the past two years undergraduate research, I did work on scenario that number of subdomains is larger or equal 3. In this case, all interfaces gather at some places called triple junction points. The existence of these points leads to lack of DOF when generating local interpolation system. This is main obstacle in multi-domain interface problems. Both spirits of classic Immersed Finite Element method and Partial Penalized Immersed Finite Element Method (T.Lin et al, 2015) are employed to adapt this new scenario. The two diagrams below shows numerical solution of specific 2D elliptic interface problems under condition of multi-domain & triple junction points and discontinuous jump condition respectively.

Currently, I’m working on Elastodynamics Interface problems, the time-dependent Elastics Interface problems. Elastodynamics has wide applications in earthquake wave modeling, in the project, we consider multi-domain Elastodynamics. We propose Full discrete Partially Penalized Immersed Finite Element scheme for this case.

Environmental Science Topics

In environmental science, applied mathematics and statistical techniques are employed to extract conclusions. Simulations based on PDEs are used to model trend of water flow, so as the transportation of pollutants. With Finite Element and Finite Volume Method, we are able to predict and formulate precaution before bad things happen. Diagram below shows kinetic energy density distribution in Binhu Network, Lake Taihu, China under condition of a water transferring project from 2018.8.1 to 2018.8.31 computed with Finite Volume Algorithm. We can also leverage information from experimental data with statistical techniques. Logarithmic Mean Divisia Index (LMDI) model is used for analyzing contributions of economic growth, population growth, energy intensity, energy structure and emission coefficient factor to carbon emission from corresponding data sets. In the past two years, I focused on these techniques on quantifying relationship between environmental impact and people reaction for integrated solutions of sustainability development.
